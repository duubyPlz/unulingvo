== Adverbs Two ==
* a -> all

> One
    O: 까치는 시끄럽습니다.
    E: Magpies are noisy. @formal
    F: _Magpies_ are noisy. @formal

    O: 한국은 조용해요?
    E: Is Korea quiet? @polite
    F: Is _Korea_ quiet? @polite

    O: 내일은 너무 늦어요!
    E: Tomorrow is too late! @polite
    F: _Tomorrow_ is too late! @polite

    O: 영화가 즐거워요.
    E: The movie is enjoyable. @polite
    F: The movie _is enjoyable_. @polite

    O: 즐겁게 공부해요.
    E: (I) study joyfully. @polite

    O: 아기가 시끄럽게 울어요.
    E: The baby cries noisily. @polite
    F: The baby _cries noisily_. @polite

    O: 게가 물에서 편하게 잡니다.
    E: The crab sleeps{ss} comfortably{l} in the water. @formal
    F: The crab _sleeps{ss} comfortably{l} in the water_. @formal

    # 조용하게 하세요 - [Lit. Do something 'quietly' (by not making noise/in a discreet manner)] Keep the noise down (Active)
    # 조용히 하세요 - [Lit. Do something 'staidly' (in a calm/boring way)] - Stay/Be quiet (Passive- Stative)
    # In daily usage, the 2 expressions have become interchangeable
    O: 조용하게 하세요.
    E: Do it quietly{l}. @polite

    O: 여자아이가 학교에 늦게 가요.
    E: The girl goes to school late. @polite
    F: The girl _goes to school late_. @polite

    O: 남자가 라디오를 들으면서 조용하게 노래해요.
    E: The man sings quietly{l} while listening to the radio. @polite
    F: The man _sings quietly{l} while listening to the radio_. @polite

    O: 아이들이 시끄럽게 놀아요.
    E: The children play noisily. @polite
    F: The children _play noisily_. @polite

    O: 늦게 자지 마세요.
    E: Do not go to sleep{ss} late. @polite

    O: 웃는 아이는 즐겁게 놀아요.
    E: The smiling child plays joyfully. @polite
    F: _The smiling child_ plays joyfully. @polite

    O: 편하게 앉으세요.
    E: Sit comfortably{l}. @polite

> Two
    O: 저는 가방 없이 학교에 가요.
    E: I go to school without a bag. @polite
    F: _I_ go to school without a bag. @polite

    O: 거북이가 토끼를 우연히 만납니다.
    E: The turtle meets the rabbit by accident. @formal
    F: The turtle _meets the rabbit by accident_. @formal

    # According to National Institute of Korean Language, 우리 and 우리들 are all okay. The same meaning, and both words are plural. I mean, as you might know already, the plural form is not really considered in Korean.
    # In my opinion, however, 우리들 is redundancy. 우리 is better.
    O: 우리들은 따로 가요.
    E: We{a} go separately. @polite
    F: _We{a}_ go separately. @polite

    O: 말이 열심히 일해요.
    E: The horse works hard. @polite
    F: The horse _works hard_. @polite

    O: 말이 조용히 달려요.
    E: The horse runs quietly. @polite
    F: The horse _runs quietly_. @polite

    O: 그 접시는 완전히 깨끗합니다.
    E: That dish is completely clean. @formal
    F: _That dish_ is completely clean. @formal

    O: 그녀는 열심히 공부하지 않아요.
    E: She does not study hard. @polite
    F: _She_ does not study hard. @polite

    # 소고기 없이 불고기를 못 만들어요. (different F: though)
    O: 소고기 없이 불고기는 못 만들어요.
    E: (We) can't make bulgogi without beef. @polite
    F: (We) can't make _bulgogi without beef_. @polite

    O: 저는 따로 놉니다.
    E: I play separately. @formal
    F: _I_ play separately. @formal

    O: 저는 책 없이 못 살아요.
    E: I cannot live without books. @polite
    F: _I_ cannot live without books. @polite

    O: 새가 조용히 편지를 써요.
    E: The bird quietly writes a letter. @polite
    F: The bird _quietly writes a letter_. @polite

    O: 도서관에서 조용히 말해요.
    E: Let's speak quietly in the library. @polite

    O: 우리는 우연히 여기에 있습니까?
    E: Are we here by chance? @formal
    F: Are _we_ here by chance? @formal

    O: 따로 요리하세요.
    E: Cook separately. @polite

    O: 이것은 완전히 달라요.
    E: This is completely different. @polite
    F: _This_ is completely different. @polite
