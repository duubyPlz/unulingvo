== Honorifics ==
* s -> short
* n -> normal
* h -> humble

# 우리: neutral
# 저희: humble

> One
    O: 김치와 드셔보세요!
    E: Try it with kimchi! @polite

    O: 우리 할아버지께서 일하세요.
    E: My grandfather works. @polite

    O: 할아버님께서 파스타를 드십니까?
    E: Does grandfather eat pasta? @formal

    O: 할머님께서 진지를 드세요.
    E: Grandmother eats rice. @polite

    O: 우리들의 할머님께서 노래하십니다.
    E: Our grandmother sings. @formal

    O: 우리 할아버지께서 일하세요.
    E: My grandfather{n} works. @polite

    O: 할머님께서 진지를 요리하세요.
    E: Grandmother cooks rice. @polite

    O: 할머님과 할아버님께서는 진지를 드십니다.
    E: Grandmother and grandfather eat rice. @formal
    F: _Grandmother and grandfather_ eat rice. @formal

    O: 할아버님께서 파스타를 드십니까?
    E: Does grandfather eat pasta? @formal

> Two
    O: 할머니께서 주무십니까?
    E: Does grandmother{n} sleep? @formal

    # 드리다: used when the recipient is respected
    # 주시다: person giving is being respected
    # 주다: normal
    O: 친구는 선생님께 책을 드립니다.
    E: The friend gives the teacher a book. @formal
    F: _The friend_ gives the teacher a book. @formal

    O: 저는 선생님께 사과를 드려요.  
    E: I give the teacher an apple. @polite
    F: _I_ give the teacher an apple. @polite

    O: 우리 할아버님께서는 주무십니다.
    E: My grandfather sleeps. @formal
    F: _My grandfather_ sleeps. @formal

    O: 저희 할아버지께서는 댁에 계십니다.
    E: My{h} grandfather{n} is at home. @formal
    F: _My{h} grandfather{n}_ is at home. @formal

    # Why is 이 used as a subject marker here when we are told 께서 is the honorific equivalent?
    # Why is it 제 선생님 here instead of 우리 선생님?
    # Yes, it is for all collectives. But, this lesson should be using 저희 instead of 우리 if it's for super formal honorifics. Funnily enough, they did use it ONCE.
    # My teacher is at school today
    # 오늘 제 선생님이 학교에 계십니다.
    O: 오늘 제 선생님께서 학교에 계십니다.
    E: Today{s}, my{s} teacher is at school. @formal

    O: 할머니께서 우리에게 천천히 말씀하십니다.
    E: Grandmother{n} speaks to us slowly. @formal

    # 할머님의 댁이 커요.
    O: 할머님의 댁께서 커요.
    E: Grandmother's house is big. @polite

    O: 저는 선생님께 사과를 드려요.
    E: I give the teacher an apple. @polite
    F: _I_ give the teacher an apple. @polite

    O: 우리들의 할머니께서 어디 계세요?
    E: Where{s} is our grandmother{n}? @polite

    O: 할아버지께서 차에서 주무십니다.
    E: Grandfather{n} sleeps in the car{s}. @formal

    O: 저는 할머니의 댁에 가요.
    E: I go to (my) grandmother{n}'s house. @polite
    F: _I_ go to (my) grandmother{n}'s house. @polite

    O: 할머니께 사과를 드리세요.
    E: Give your grandmother{n} an apple. @polite

    O: 할머님께서 할아버님께 말씀하세요.
    E: Grandmother speaks to Grandfather. @polite

    O: 선생님께서 학생에게 말씀하십니다.
    E: The teacher talks to the student. @formal

> Three
    O: 우리 할아버님의 성함은 가람이에요.
    E: My grandfather's name is Garam. @polite
    F: _My grandfather's name_ is Garam. @polite

    O: 선생님 네 분
    E: Four teachers

    # 저는 할아버지 생신 케이크를 삽니다.
    # ^ "I buy a birthday cake for grandfathers." Or maybe "cake for grandfathers' birthdays."
    O: 저는 할아버지께 생신 케이크를 삽니다.
    E: I buy a birthday cake for grandfather{n}. @formal
    F: _I_ buy a birthday cake for grandfather{n}. @formal

    O: 우리 할아버지는 연세가 많습니다.
    E: My grandfather{n} is old. @formal
    F: _My grandfather{n}_ is old. @formal

    O: 우리 할머니의 연세가 어떻게 되세요?
    E: How old is my grandmother{n}? @polite

    O: 오늘은 선생님 두 분의 생신이에요.
    E: It is two teachers' birthday today. @polite
    F: It is two teachers' birthday _today_. @polite

    # 나이가 어떻게 되세요?
    # 몇 살이에요? # non-honorific
    # 연세가 어떻게 되세요?
    O: 몇살 이세요?
    E: How old are you? (Less honorific) @polite

    # 신사: gentleman, 신부: lady
    O: 이분의 성함은 어떻게 되세요?
    E: What is this gentleman/lady's name? @polite
    F: What is _this gentleman/lady's name_? @polite

    O: 연세가 어떻게 되세요?
    E: How old are you? (More honorific) @polite

    O: 오늘은 할머님의 생신이에요.
    E: Today is Grandmother's birthday. @polite
    F: _Today_ is Grandmother's birthday. @polite

    O: 저는 제 선생님 성함을 몰라요.
    E: I do not know my teacher's name. @polite
    F: _I_ do not know my{s} teacher's{s} name. @polite

    O: 제 할아버지는 연세가 많습니다.
    E: My{s} grandfather{n} is old. @formal
    F: _My{s} grandfather{n}_ is old. @formal
