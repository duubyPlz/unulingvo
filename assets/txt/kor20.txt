== Continuous Verbs ==
* s -> short

> One
    O: 공원이 지도에 있습니다.
    E: The park is on the map. @formal
    F: The park _is on the map_. @formal

    # Hello! Thank you! Take care! See you again!
    O: 안녕하세요! 감사해요! 잘 있어요! 다시 만나요!
    E: Hello! Thank you! Be well! Meet you again! @polite

    O: 선생님이 앉아 있어요.
    E: The teacher is sitting. @polite
    F: The teacher _is sitting_. @polite

    O: 선생님은 지도를 보고 있습니다.  
    E: The teacher is looking at a map. @formal
    F: _The teacher_ is looking at a map. @formal

    O: 게으른 회사원은 자고 있습니다.
    E: The lazy employee is sleeping{ss}. @formal
    F: _The lazy employee_ is sleeping{ss}. @formal

    O: 회사원은 라디오를 가져요.
    E: The employee takes a radio. @polite
    F: _The employee_ takes a radio. @polite

    O: 게으른 개가 천천히 먹고 있어요.
    E: The lazy dog is eating slowly. @polite
    F: The lazy dog _is eating slowly_. @polite

    O: 게으른 선생님은 텔레비전을 보고 있어요.
    E: The lazy teacher is watching television. @polite
    F: _The lazy teacher_ is watching television. @polite

    O: 회사원이 김밥을 가지고 있어요.
    E: The employee has gimbap. @polite
    F: The employee _has gimbap_. @polite

    O: 한국이 지도에 없습니다.
    E: Korea is not on the map. @formal
    F: Korea _is not on the map_. @formal

    O: 여우가 쥐를 가지고 있어요.
    E: The fox has a mouse. @polite
    F: The fox _has a mouse_. @polite

> Two
    O: 빈 의자
    E: Empty chair

    O: 무거운 와인을 조심하십시오!
    E: Watch out for the heavy wine! @formal

    O: 예쁜 아기는 많이 울지 않아요.
    E: Pretty babies don't cry much. @polite
    F: _Pretty babies_ don't cry much. @polite

    O: 제 친구가 피자를 들고 있습니다.
    E: My{s} friend is carrying a pizza. @formal
    F: My{s} friend _is carrying a pizza_. @formal

    O: 이 가벼운 상자 안에 무엇이 있습니까?
    E: What's in this light box? @formal

    O: 생각하는 회사원은 천천히 일하고 있어요.
    E: The thinking employee is working slowly. @polite
    F: _The thinking employee_ is working slowly. @polite

    # 앉는 going from standing to sitting
    # 앉는 여자가 생각하고 있어요.
    O: 앉아 있는 여자가 생각하고 있어요.
    E: The woman sitting is thinking. @polite
    F: The woman sitting_ is thinking_. @polite

    # 저는 앉는 남자하고 이야기하고 있어요.
    O: 저는 앉아 있는 남자하고 이야기하고 있어요.
    E: I am talking with the seated man. @polite
    F: _I_ am talking with the seated man. @polite

    O: 집은 비어 있습니까?
    E: Is _the house_ empty? @formal

    O: 가벼운 텔레비전
    E: A light television

    O: 예쁜 나비가 날고 있어요.
    E: The pretty butterfly is flying. @polite
    F: The pretty butterfly _is flying_. @polite

    O: 예쁜 여자가 수영하고 있어요.
    E: The pretty woman is swimming. @polite
    F: The pretty woman _is swimming_. @polite

    O: 똑똑한 학생이 책 네 권을 들고 있어요.
    E: The smart student is carrying four books. @polite
    F: The smart student _is carrying four books_. @polite

    O: 우리는 무거운 책을 읽고 있어요.
    E: We are reading a heavy book. @polite
    F: _We_ are reading a heavy book. @polite

    # 서다 is a stative verb, which has the irregular progressive form of appending the 해체 form with 있다. Thus the appropriate present progressive verb is 서 있다.
    # Note: can still form a progressive verb in the usual fashion (서고 있다) but the meaning shifts to "in the process of standing".
    O: 생각하는 여자가 서 있어요.
    E: The thinking woman is standing. @polite
    F: The thinking woman _is standing_. @polite

    O: 생각하는 선생님은 책을 읽고 있어요.
    E: The thinking teacher is reading a book. @polite
    F: _The thinking teacher_ is reading a book. @polite
