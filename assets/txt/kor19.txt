== Compound Verbs ==
* m -> my
* sp -> spoken
* s -> short

> One
    O: 남자가 돌려줍니다.
    E: The man returns it. @formal
    F: The man _returns it_. @formal

    O: 남자에게 자동차를 빌려주세요.
    E: Lend the man a car. @polite

    O: 저는 친구에게 연필을 빌려줍니다.
    E: I lend a pencil to my friend. @formal
    F: _I_ lend a pencil to my friend. @formal

    O: 이 음식을 먹어보세요.
    E: Try this food please. @polite

    O: 아이는 큰 탁자를 빌려요.
    E: A child borrows a big table. @polite
    F: _A child_ borrows a big table. @polite

    O: 그는 소를 도와주지 않아요.

    F: _He_ does not help the cow. @polite

    F: The students _help each other_. @polite

    # With regard to the compound verbs, as McPwny mentioned, one is active and the other passive. Essentially, if there is an agent acting on an object (i.e. a dog returning a ball) then the compound verb will contain the root 돌려. If there is no agent acting on the object/subject (i.e. a car comes back) then you use the root 돌아
    O: 개가 공을 돌려줍니다.
    E: The dog returns the ball. @formal
    F: The dog _returns the ball_. @formal

    O: 조용히 생각을 해보세요.
    E: Try to think quietly. @polite

    F: The child _is afraid of the monster_. @polite

    O: 슬퍼하지 마십시오.
    E: Don't be sad. @formal

    # 남자아이 is the subject of the verb 없다, so it has the subject marker
    # 개 is the subject of 슬퍼하다 but is also the main topic of the sentence, so it has the 는 marker
    O: 남자아이가 없으면 개는 슬퍼해요.
    E: If the boy is not there, the dog is sad. @polite
    F: If the boy is not there, _the dog_ is sad. @polite

    O: 큰 부엉이가 슬퍼합니다.
    E: The big owl is sad. @formal
    F: The big owl _is sad_. @formal

    O: 저는 제 자신을 무서워해요.
    E: I am afraid of myself[m]. @polite
    F: _I_ am afraid of myself[m]. @polite

    O: 괴물이 어디에 있습니까?
    E: Where is the monster? @formal
    F: _Where is_ the monster? @formal

    O: 나는 숲 속의 호랑이를 무서워해요.
    E: I'm afraid of the tiger in the forest. @polite,i
    F: _I_'m afraid of the tiger in the forest. @polite,i

    O: 저는 한국에 가고 싶어요.
    E: I want to go to Korea. @polite
    F: _I_ want to go to Korea. @polite

    O: 긴 뱀은 아주 무서워요.

    O: 여자아이가 날고 싶어해요.

    # "probable future", add ~겠다
    # ~ㄹ 거예요 is a "for sure" or "definite" kind of feeling like
    # 좋다(be good) becomes 좋겠다(likely to be good)
    # 알다 and 모르다 are often conjugated to 알겠다 or 모르겠다.
    # Although they are conjugated to the future tense, those two words are typically used to express _ in the present tense.
    O: 나는 돌아오겠다.
    E: I'll be back. @plain,i
    F: _I_'ll be back. @plain,i
