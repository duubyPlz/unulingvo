== TTMIK 2.25 - Something, Someday, Someone, Somewhere / 누군가, 무언가, 어딘가, 언젠가 ==
* s -> short

    O: 누군가
    E: someone

    O: 뭔가
    E: something

    O: 어딘가
    E: somewhere

    O: 언젠가
    E: someday

    O: 언젠가 미국에 가고 싶어요.
    E: (I) want to go to the United States someday.

    O: 언제 미국에 가고 싶어요?
    E: When do you want to go to the United States?

    O: 언젠가 일본에 갈 거예요.
    E: (I) am going to go to Japan one day.

    O: 언제 일본에 갈 거예요?
    E: When are you going to go to Japan?

    O: 뭐 찾았어요?
    E: What did you find?

    O: 뭔가 찾았어요?
    E: Did you find something?

    O: 뭔가 이상해요.
    E: Something is strange.

    O: 뭐가 이상해요?
    E: What is strange?
    F: What _is strange?_

    O: 누구 만날 거예요?
    E: Whom will you meet?

    O: 누군가 만날 거예요?
    E: Will you meet someone?

    O: 누군가 왔어요.
    E: Someone came.

    O: 어디에 있어요?
    E: Where is it?

    O: 여기 어딘가에 있어요.
    E: It is here somewhere.

    # (stress is on 뭐)
    O: 뭐 샀어요?
    E: What did you buy?

    # (stress is on 샀어요)
    O: 뭐 샀어요?
    E: Did you buy something[s]? 

    # (stress is on 언제)
    O: 언제 중국에 갈 거예요?
    E: When[s] are you going to go to China?

    # (stress is on 갈 거예요?)
    O: 언제 중국에 갈 거예요?
    E: Are you going to go to China someday[s]/one of these days?

    # (stress is on 어디)
    O: 어디 가요?
    E: Where[s] are you going?

    # (stress is on 가요?)
    O: 어디 가요?
    E: Are you going somewhere[s]?

    # (stress is on 배웠어요?)
    O: 오늘 뭐 배웠어요?
    E: Did you learn something[s] today?

    # (stress is on 뭐)
    O: 오늘 뭐 배웠어요?
    E: What did you learn today?
