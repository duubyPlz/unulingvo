== TTMIK 2.22 - To like / 좋다 vs 좋아하다 ==
* s -> short

    # <subject> 좋다 - to personally like
    # <object> 좋다 - to be good
    # <object> 좋아하다 - to like + 3rd party (likes)

    O: 이 가수가 좋아요.
    E: I (personally) like this singer.

    O: 이 가수를 좋아요.
    E: This singer is good.

    O: 한국어가 좋아요.
    E: I (personally) like Korean.
    F: _I (personally) like_ Korean.

    O: 한국어 좋아요.
    E: I (personally) like[s] Korean.

    O: 싫다
    E: to be unlikable; to be undesirable

    O: 싫어하다
    E: to hate; to not like

    O: 예뻐하다
    E: to consider someone pretty and treat them in such a manner

    O: 슬퍼하다
    E: to feel sad and therefore express such emotions

    O: 슬퍼하지 마세요.
    E: Do not be sad.

    O: 싫어하지 마세요.
    E: Do not hate me.

    O: 저는 우유를 좋아해요.
    E: I like milk.

    O: 저는 우유를 안 좋아해요.
    E: I do not like milk.

    O: 우유가 좋아요? 주스가 좋아요?
    E: Do you (personally) like milk? (Or) Do you (personally) like juice?
    F: _Do you (personally) like_ milk_? (Or) Do you (personally) like_ juice?

    O: 뭐가 제일 좋아요?
    E: What is your (personal) favorite?

    O: 뭐를 제일 좋아해요? 
    E: What do you like best?

    O: 경은 씨는 송중기를 좋아해요.
    E: Kyeong-eun likes Song Joong-ki.

    O: 한국 영화 좋아하세요?
    E: Do you like Korean movies[s]? @honorific

    O: 저 좋아하세요?
    E: Do you like me[s]? / Are you in love with me? @honorific
