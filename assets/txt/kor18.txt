== Adverbs Two ==
* p -> plural

> One
    O: 까치는 시끄럽습니다.
    E: Magpies are noisy. @formal
    F: _Magpies_ are noisy. @formal

    O: 한국은 조용해요?
    E: Is Korea quiet? @polite
    F: Is _Korea_ quiet? @polite

    O: 내일은 너무 늦어요!
    E: Tomorrow is too late! @polite
    F: _Tomorrow_ is too late! @polite

    O: 영화가 즐거워요.
    E: The movie is enjoyable. @polite
    F: The movie _is enjoyable_. @polite

    O: 즐겁게 공부해요.
    E: (I) study joyfully. @polite

    O: 아기가 시끄럽게 울어요.
    E: The baby cries noisily. @polite
    F: The baby _cries noisily_. @polite

    O: 게가 물에서 편하게 잡니다.
    E: The crab sleeps[ss] comfortably in the water. @formal
    F: The crab _sleeps[ss] comfortably in the water_. @formal

    # 조용하게 하세요 - [Lit. Do something 'quietly' (by not making noise/in a discreet manner)] Keep the noise down (Active)
    # 조용히 하세요 - [Lit. Do something 'staidly' (in a calm/boring way)] - Stay/Be quiet (Passive- Stative)
    # In daily usage, the 2 expressions have become interchangeable
    O: 조용하게 하세요.
    E: Do it quietly. @polite

    O: 여자아이가 학교에 늦게 가요.
    E: The girl goes to school late. @polite
    F: The girl _goes to school late_. @polite

    O: 남자가 라디오를 들으면서 조용하게 노래해요.
    E: The man sings quietly while listening to the radio. @polite
    F: The man _sings quietly while listening to the radio_. @polite

    O: 아이들이 시끄럽게 놀아요.
    E: The children play noisily. @polite
    F: The children _play noisily_. @polite

    O: 늦게 자지 마세요.
    E: Do not go to sleep[ss] late. @polite

    O: 웃는 아이는 즐겁게 놀아요.
    E: The smiling child plays joyfully. @polite
    F: _The smiling child_ plays joyfully. @polite

    O: 편하게 앉으세요.
    E: Sit comfortably. @polite
