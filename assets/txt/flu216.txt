== TTMIK 2.16 - Very, A bit, Really, Not really, Not at all / 아주, 조금, 진짜, 별로, 전혀 ==
* sp -> spoken

    # 조금 = a little, a bit, a little bit
    # 정말 = really, truly
    # 아주 = very, quite
        # // 아주 standard written form, spoken 아주 replaced with 정말 or 진짜.
    # 별로 = not really, not particularly
    # 전혀 = not at all

    # 조금, 아주, and 정말 used with any sentence
    # 별로 and 전혀 only used with negative sentences.

    # ^ Intensity. 정말 and 진짜 just to express true.
    # // 진짜 considered a little less formal than 정말

    O: 조금 비싸요.
    E: It is a little expensive.

    O: 조금만 주세요.
    E: Give (me) only a little bit.

    O: 물 조금만 주세요.
    E: Give (me) only a little bit of water.

    O: 소금 조금만 주세요.
    E: Give (me) only a little bit of salt.

    O: 정말 빨라요. 
    E: It is really fast.

    O: 정말 이상해요.
    E: It is really strange.

    O: 제가 방 청소했어요!
    E: I cleaned up my room!
    F: I _cleaned up my room_!

    O: 아... 진짜?
    E: Oh... really[sp]?

    O: 예, 진짜! 보고 싶어요?
    E: Yeah, really[sp]! Do you want to see it?

    O: 아주 맛있어요.
    E: It is very delicious.

    O: 아주 멀어요
    E: It is very far away.

    O: 별로 안 비싸요.
    E: It is not so expensive.

    O: 별로 재미없어요.
    E: It is not that interesting.

    O: 별로 안 나빠요.
    E: It is not too bad. (It is not the worst, but it is still bad.)

    O: 전혀 안 바빠요.
    E: I am not busy at all.

    O: 전혀 안 더워요.
    E: It is not hot at all.

    O: 하나도 안 더워요.
    E: It is not hot at all[sp].
