    # 곧 ... 봐요: see you soon

    E: See you soon in Seoul!

    O: 미안해요. 우리 곧 다시 만나요.
    E: I am sorry. Let's[u] meet again soon.

    # Let’s[u] hide there!
    O: 우리 저기에 숨어요!
    E: Let's[u] hide over there!

    # 다니다 is almost of like "attend" but you will find it in a lot of compound verbs like 단녀오다 and 단녀가다 to visit and then come/go, and others like:
    #    * 찾아다니다 to go searching around
    #    * 뛰어다니다 to jump or run around
    #    * 떠돌아다니다 to wander around etc
    # The Korean answer here implies that it's occurring frequently, whereas the English is inferring a one-off journey.
    O: 개하고 도서관에 다녀요.
    E: Let's go[r] to the library with the dog.

    O: 내일 제 친구의 집에서 놀아요!
    E: Let's hang out at my[s] friend's house tomorrow!
