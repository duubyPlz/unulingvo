== TTMIK 4.14 - Always, Often, Sometimes, Rarely, Almost Never, Never / 항상, 맨날, 자주, 가끔, 별로, 거의, 전혀 ==
* s -> short
* sp -> spoken
* wr -> written
* l -> long

    # {#RELATED Level 2 Lesson 16}: 아주, 정말, 진짜, 조금, 별로, 전혀

    # 빈도 부사 (頻度 副詞): Frequency adverbs
    # * Meanings:
    #   * 항상: Always
    #   * 맨날: Always (lit: every day), spoken
    #   * 자주: Often
    #   * 가끔: Sometimes
    #   * 별로: Rarely
    #   * 거의: Almost [always|never]
    #   * 전혀: Never

    O: 가끔 서점에 가요.
    E: Sometimes, I go to the bookstore.
    F: _Sometimes,_ I go to the bookstore.

    O: 서점에 가끔 가요.
    E: I go to the bookstore sometimes.
    F: _I go to the bookstore_ sometimes.

    O: 자주 한국 영화 봐요.
    E: Often, I watch Korean movies.
    F: _Often,_ I watch Korean movies.

    O: 한국 영화 자주 봐요.
    E: I often watch Korean movies.
    F: _I watch Korean movies_ often.

    O: 그런 말 정말 자주 들어요.
    E: I hear that[s] really often. / I get that really often.

    O: 항상 물어보고 싶었어요.
    E: I've always[wr] wanted to ask you (that).

    O: 중국어를 맨날 공부하는데, 아직 어려워요.
    E: I study Chinese all the time[sp], but it is still[s] difficult.

    O: 요즘에는 운동을 별로 안 해요.
    E: I don't really work out[l] these days[l]. / I rarely work out these days.
    F: I don't really work out[l] _these days[l]_. / I rarely work out these days.

    # I never put on makeup[l].
    O: 화장을 전혀 안 해요.
    E: I never put makeup[l] on.

    O: 시간이 없어서, 친구들을 거의 못 만나요.
    E: I do not have time[l], so I almost can never meet my friends[l].

    O: 라디오를 거의 맨날 들어요.
    E: I almost always[sp] listen to the radio.
